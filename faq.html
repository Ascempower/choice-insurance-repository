Medicare Questions

Original Medicare (Parts A and B) is the traditional fee-for-service program run by the federal government. Medicare Advantage (Part C) is an alternative way to receive Medicare benefits through private insurance companies approved by Medicare. Medicare Advantage plans often include prescription drug coverage and may offer additional benefits like dental, vision, or wellness programs.

Medicare Supplement insurance, also called Medigap, is a private insurance policy that helps pay for some of the healthcare costs that Original Medicare doesn't cover, such as copayments, coinsurance, and deductibles. These plans are standardized and labeled with letters (Plan A, Plan B, Plan C, etc.), with each plan offering the same basic benefits regardless of which insurance company sells it.

Your Initial Enrollment Period begins 3 months before you turn 65 and ends 3 months after your 65th birthday month. You can also enroll during the General Enrollment Period (January 1 - March 31) or during Special Enrollment Periods if you qualify. For Medicare Advantage and Part D plans, there's an Annual Open Enrollment Period from October 15 - December 7.

Life Insurance Questions

The amount of life insurance you need depends on your financial obligations, income, debts, and family situation. A common rule of thumb is 10-12 times your annual income, but we recommend a personalized analysis considering your mortgage, children's education costs, final expenses, and your family's ongoing living expenses. Our agents can help you calculate the right amount for your specific situation.

Term life insurance provides coverage for a specific period (10, 20, or 30 years) and is typically more affordable. Whole life insurance provides permanent coverage with a cash value component that grows over time. Term life is ideal for temporary needs like mortgage protection, while whole life offers lifelong protection and can serve as an investment vehicle.
